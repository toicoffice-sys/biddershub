How to Use BiddersHub

1. Browse bid opportunities — no account needed

Anyone can view the public bid board. Use the search box and category filter to narrow the list. Click any bid card for full details: description, estimated budget, submission deadline, attached documents, and published questions & answers.

Each bid has a reference number (e.g. ITB-2026-0001) — quote this when contacting the CPD about a specific bid.

2. Become an accredited vendor

Click Become an Accredited Vendor in the top navigation. First, enter your email and verify it with the 6-digit code sent to you — this happens before anything else, so no document can be uploaded under an unverified address. Once verified, fill in your company name, trade name, business category, TIN number, DTI/SEC registration number, contact person, contact number, and business address.

Then upload your accreditation documents — one PDF file per document, not a bulk upload:

Files must be PDF only, max 10MB each. For security, you verify your email with a 6-digit code before you can upload anything or fill in the form. Once submitted, your status starts as Pending.

3. Signing in

Click Login, enter your email, then enter the 6-digit code sent to it. If your email isn't recognized, you'll be prompted to apply for accreditation instead.

4. Checking your accreditation status

Once signed in, the Accreditation Status tab shows your current status, stamped like an office document:

  • Pending — submitted, awaiting CPD review
  • Approved — accredited; your accreditation number and validity date appear here
  • Rejected — see the reviewer's notes; you may re-apply with corrected documents

5. Browsing & asking questions

Under Browse & Inquire, open any published bid and use Ask a question to submit an inquiry to the CPD. Track your questions and answers under My Inquiries — once answered, the Q&A also appears publicly on that bid's page (without your name) so other vendors benefit too.

6. Submitting a bid

You must be accredited (status: Approved) before you can submit a bid. Open any Published bid and scroll to Submit Your Bid. If you're not yet accredited, you'll see your current status instead of an upload form. Once accredited, upload a Technical Proposal and Financial Proposal / Price Quotation (both required PDFs), plus any Other Supporting Documents (optional). You can replace a document and resubmit as many times as you like before the deadline — only your latest submission counts.

Troubleshooting

  • Didn't receive the code? Check spam/junk, then use Resend code (codes expire after 10 minutes; you can request a new one after 60 seconds).
  • "An accreditation record already exists"? You already have a Pending or Approved application — contact the CPD to make changes.
  • Can't upload a document? Confirm it's a PDF file under 10MB.

Bid opportunity workflow

Draft → Pending Approval → Approved → Published → Closed

  1. Draft — CPD staff creates a bid opportunity (title, category, department, budget, deadline, description, and bid document templates — RFQ/ITB and Terms of Reference are required). Save as draft or submit immediately.
  2. Submit for Approval — moves the bid to the Approvals tab. Blocked until required documents are attached.
  3. Approve / Reject — CPD reviews. Approve moves it to Approved; Reject sends it back to Draft with a reason.
  4. Publish — makes the bid visible on the public bid board, open for accredited vendors to submit bids and ask questions, and starts the KPI turnaround clock.
  5. Close — once the deadline passes, close with an outcome: Awarded, Cancelled, or No Award. Closed bids stay visible for transparency and audit.

Reviewing bid submissions

Only accredited (Approved) vendors can submit a bid — checked live at submission time, not just at login. On a Published or Closed bid, click View Submissions to see every vendor's Technical Proposal, Financial Proposal, and any other documents they attached, in submission order.

Reviewing vendor accreditation

The Vendor Accreditation tab lists applications with each applicant's uploaded PDF documents as clickable links — review them before deciding on a Pending application. Approve assigns a permanent accreditation number and 1-year validity. Request Changes is for incomplete or incorrect submissions — it requires a note, emails the vendor automatically, and lets them replace just the affected documents without a full re-application. Use Resend Reminder if they haven't acted yet. Reject is a hard stop requiring a reason — the vendor would need to start a fresh application to try again.

Responding to inquiries

The Inquiries tab lists vendor questions on published bids. Once answered, the Q&A becomes visible to all vendors on that bid's public page — helps everyone considering bidding, not just the one who asked.

KPI Dashboard

Live metrics computed from the sheets: % of bids published within 1 business day of approval, average approval-to-publish turnaround, bid counts by status, accredited/pending vendor counts, and open inquiry counts.

Managing staff users (Admin only)

The Staff Users tab lets an Administrator add staff (email, name, role, department) or deactivate an account without deleting its history.

Audit log (Admin only)

Every login, create, approve, publish, close, and review action is logged with actor email, role, and timestamp — satisfies the Data Privacy Act (RA 10173) data-logging requirement.
